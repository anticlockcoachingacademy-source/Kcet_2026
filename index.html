💡 KCET Cheat Codes & Exam Strategy
PHY
In Kirchhoff problems: always assign current directions first, then write loop equations systematically.
PHY
Potentiometer: "If balancing length increases → EMF of cell increased / internal resistance decreased." Remember this logic.
CHEM
Molality uses mass of solvent (kg). Molarity uses volume of solution (L). Never mix these up — most common exam trap.
CHEM
Van't Hoff factor i > 1 for electrolytes (dissociation), i < 1 for association (acetic acid in benzene).
MATH
To prove a function is NOT one-one: find two different inputs giving same output. To prove NOT onto: find an element in codomain with no pre-image.
PHY
If last band of colour code is absent → tolerance is ±20%. Gold band → ±5%. Silver → ±10%.
CHEM
Raoult's positive deviation → liquids with weaker interactions → lower BP than expected (min boiling azeotrope: ethanol-water).
MATH
Composition is NOT commutative: fog ≠ gof in general. Always compute from right to left: fog(x) = f(g(x)).

🔢 Formula Sheet – Day 1 Chapters

⚡ Current Electricity – Physics
Electric Current
I = Q / t
I in Amperes, Q in Coulombs, t in seconds
Ohm's Law
V = I × R
Applies to ohmic conductors at constant temperature
Resistivity
R = ρL / A | ρ = RA / L
ρ in Ω·m; L = length, A = cross-section area
Temperature Dependence
ρ = ρ₀(1 + α·ΔT)
α = temperature coefficient of resistance
Drift Velocity
I = nAev_d ⇒ v_d = I/(nAe)
n = charge density, e = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C
Series Resistance
R_s = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
Same current through all; voltages add up
Parallel Resistance
1/R_p = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃
Same voltage; currents add up
EMF & Terminal Voltage
V = E – I·r | E = V + I·r
r = internal resistance; I = current drawn
Kirchhoff's Current Law (KCL)
ΣI_in = ΣI_out (Junction rule)
Based on conservation of charge
Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL)
ΣV = 0 (Loop rule)
Based on conservation of energy
Electrical Power
P = VI = I²R = V²/R
P in Watts; Energy E = P×t (Joules)
Wheatstone Bridge
P/Q = R/S (balanced condition)
No current through galvanometer when balanced
Potentiometer – Internal Resistance
r = R × (l₁ – l₂) / l₂
l₁ = balancing length without shunt; l₂ = with shunt R
🧪 Solutions – Chemistry
Molarity (M)
M = n_solute / V_solution(L)
Moles per litre of solution; changes with temperature
Molality (m)
m = n_solute / W_solvent(kg)
Moles per kg of SOLVENT; temperature independent
Mole Fraction
χ₁ = n₁ / (n₁ + n₂) | χ₁ + χ₂ = 1
Dimensionless; sum of mole fractions = 1
Henry's Law
p = K_H × χ
K_H = Henry's constant; χ = mole fraction of gas
Raoult's Law
P_A = χ_A × P°_A | P_total = P_A + P_B
Ideal solutions obey Raoult's law at all concentrations
Relative Lowering of Vapour Pressure
(P° – P_s)/P° = χ_solute = n₂/(n₁+n₂)
First colligative property
Elevation in Boiling Point
ΔT_b = K_b × m
K_b = ebullioscopic constant (for water: 0.52 K·kg/mol)
Depression in Freezing Point
ΔT_f = K_f × m
K_f = cryoscopic constant (for water: 1.86 K·kg/mol)
Osmotic Pressure
π = MRT (M = molarity, R = 0.0821 L·atm·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹)
Van't Hoff equation; isotonic: π₁ = π₂
Van't Hoff Factor
i = observed colligative prop. / normal colligative prop.
i > 1: dissociation | i < 1: association
Modified Colligative Properties
ΔT_b = i × K_b × m | ΔT_f = i × K_f × m
Used for electrolytes; π = iMRT
Dilution Formula
M₁V₁ = M₂V₂
For diluting solutions; moles of solute conserved
📐 Relations & Functions – Mathematics
Reflexive Relation
(a, a) ∈ R for all a ∈ A
Every element relates to itself
Symmetric Relation
(a,b) ∈ R ⇒ (b,a) ∈ R
If a relates to b, then b relates to a
Transitive Relation
(a,b)∈R and (b,c)∈R ⇒ (a,c)∈R
Chain rule for relations
Equivalence Relation
Reflexive + Symmetric + Transitive
Must satisfy all three properties simultaneously
One-One (Injective) Function
f(x₁) = f(x₂) ⇒ x₁ = x₂
Different inputs always give different outputs
Onto (Surjective) Function
For every y ∈ B, ∃ x ∈ A: f(x) = y
Range = Codomain
Bijective Function
One-one AND Onto
Invertible; has unique inverse function
Composition of Functions
fog(x) = f(g(x)) | gof(x) = g(f(x))
Apply g first, then f (for fog); NOT commutative
Inverse Function
fof⁻¹ = I_B | f⁻¹of = I_A
Only bijective functions have inverses
Binary Operation Identity
a * e = e * a = a
e is the identity element for operation *
Number of Relations on A
2^(n²) where |A| = n
Total possible subsets of A×A

🎯 Quick Cheat Codes — Chapter Endings (Score 45+)

⚡ Physics Tricks

Colour code: BB ROY of Great Britain Very Good Wife — Black Brown Red Orange Yellow Green Blue Violet Grey White = 0–9
Parallel shortcuts: 2 equal resistors in parallel = R/2
Potentiometer: Greater length → better sensitivity. Resistance wire must be uniform.
Power bulb brighter: Higher resistance in parallel gets less current → dimmer. Higher wattage bulb = lower resistance.
KVL sign convention: Going through R in direction of current → drop is –IR

🧪 Chemistry Tricks

Molarity vs Molality: M uses solution volume; m uses solvent mass. For water: nearly equal for dilute solutions.
Azeotrope: Cannot be separated by distillation. Min boiling = positive deviation (ethanol-water). Max boiling = negative deviation (HNO₃-water).
Osmotic pressure direction: Water moves from low concentration to high concentration region.
K_f water = 1.86, K_b water = 0.52 Memorise these constants.
Isotonic solutions: Same osmotic pressure, same molar concentration.

📐 Maths Tricks

Equivalence quick-check: Reflexive→diagonal; Symmetric→if (a,b) then (b,a); Transitive→chain links.
fog vs gof: Always compute inner function first. fog(2) = f(g(2)).
Bijective test: Is it one-one? Is it onto? If both yes → invertible.
Identity for +: 0. Identity for ×: 1. Binary op on ℤ→ test both sides.
Equivalence class [a]: All elements related to a. If R = {(a,a),(b,b),(c,c)}, then [a]={a}.
